PURPOSE

  • To formally notify the employee of their termination from the company.

  • To outline the reasons for termination and any relevant details.

SCOPE

This termination letter applies to the employee whose performance and conduct have been evaluated and found unsatisfactory.

RESPONSIBILITY

The Human Resources department is responsible for ensuring the termination process is conducted fairly and in accordance with company policy.

GUIDELINES-

Ensure all company property is returned, and access to company systems is revoked.

Termination Process Steps

  • Conduct a final performance review meeting.

  • Prepare the termination letter with all necessary details.

  • Schedule a meeting to deliver the termination letter.

  • Discuss final paycheck and benefits.

PROCEDURE

Advance preparation:-

Review the employee's performance records and document any incidents leading to termination.

Termination Meeting Preparation:-

Equipment Used:- Employee records, termination letter, final paycheck details.

Procedure:- Schedule a private meeting with the employee to discuss the termination.

Final Documentation:-

Equipment Used:- Termination letter, exit interview form.

Procedure:- Ensure all documentation is signed and filed appropriately.

Access Revocation: -

Equipment Used: - IT systems, security access logs.

Procedure: -

  1. Notify IT to revoke system access.

Ensure all company property is collected.

  1. Conduct an exit interview if applicable.

  2. Provide information on final paycheck and benefits.

  3. Discuss any non-compete or confidentiality agreements.

  4. Ensure the employee understands the reasons for termination.

  5. Answer any questions the employee may have.

  6. Provide contact information for further inquiries.

  7. Thank the employee for their service.

  8. Escort the employee out of the premises if necessary.

  9. Complete all necessary HR documentation.

Post-Termination Measures

  • Update employee records to reflect termination.

  • Notify relevant departments of the termination.

  • Conduct a review of the termination process for improvements.

After Termination Activity:

  1. Ensure all company property is returned.

  2. Finalize any outstanding payments or benefits.

  3. Update payroll and benefits systems.

  4. Conduct a debrief with the HR team.

  5. Review the termination process for compliance.

  6. Document any feedback from the exit interview.

  7. Prepare a report for management review.

  8. Ensure all legal requirements are met.

  9. Maintain confidentiality of the termination process.

ENVIRONMENT AND OH&S REQUIREMENTS

  • Ensure a safe and respectful environment during the termination process.

  • Maintain confidentiality and privacy of the employee's information.

  • Adhere to all legal and company policies regarding termination.

  • Provide support and resources for the employee if needed.

  • Ensure all safety protocols are followed during the process.

  • Conduct the termination in a private and secure location.

  • Ensure all communication is clear and professional.

  • Provide a contact for any post-termination queries.

  • Document the process for future reference.

Required Documentation for Termination

  • Termination letter signed by HR and management.

  • Final paycheck and benefits summary.

  • Exit interview form if applicable.

  • Non-compete and confidentiality agreements.

  • Employee's performance records.

  • Access revocation confirmation.

  • Return of company property form.

  • HR documentation checklist.

  • Management review report.

  • POSITION IMPLEMENTING FUNCTIONS

  • HR Manager, IT Support, Security Personnel

Ensure all steps are followed to maintain a professional and compliant termination process.
